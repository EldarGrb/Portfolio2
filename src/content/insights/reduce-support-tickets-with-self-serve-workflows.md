## Problem Statement
Support teams spend too much time answering the same low-complexity requests. As volume grows, response times slip, senior staff get pulled into simple tasks, and urgent customer issues wait longer than they should.

## Symptoms Checklist
- More than 30% of tickets are repeat questions with the same answer.
- New agents rely on tribal knowledge and internal chats to solve basic cases.
- First-response times increase during launches, migrations, or billing cycles.
- Escalation channels are flooded with requests that should be resolved in under 5 minutes.

## Root Causes
- Existing documentation is scattered across tools and rarely updated.
- Ticket forms collect too little context, so agents ask follow-up questions.
- There is no decision-tree logic that routes users to the right fix path.
- Teams do not track which repeat issues should be converted into self-serve guidance.

## Step-by-Step Solution
1. Audit the top 20 recurring ticket types from the last 90 days and rank by volume and handling time.
2. Build a guided intake form that asks for version, environment, and error context before submission.
3. Map each repeat issue to a decision tree with clear yes/no branches and concrete next actions.
4. Publish short resolution playbooks with screenshots, expected outcomes, and fallback paths.
5. Add deflection analytics to measure when users self-resolve without creating a ticket.
6. Review weekly ticket tags to promote newly recurring issues into the self-serve library.

## Time and Error Savings Estimate
A typical team handling 1,200 monthly tickets can deflect 18-30% of low-complexity volume in the first 60 days. That usually saves 55-90 agent hours per month, reduces handoff mistakes, and frees specialist time for high-impact incidents.

## FAQ
### How quickly can we launch the first self-serve version?
Most teams can release a usable first version in 2-3 weeks if they focus on the highest-volume ticket categories first.

### Should we hide contact options once self-serve is live?
No. Keep a clear escalation path so users can still reach support when the workflow does not match their case.

### What KPI should we watch first?
Start with ticket deflection rate, then track first-response time and customer satisfaction after rollout.
