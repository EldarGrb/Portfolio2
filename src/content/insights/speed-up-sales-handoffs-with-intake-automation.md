## Problem Statement
Sales-to-delivery handoffs often stall because critical project details are collected too late. Delivery teams then spend kickoff meetings re-gathering basics instead of planning execution.

## Symptoms Checklist
- Kickoff meetings reveal missing scope, timeline, or stakeholder details.
- Sales notes are inconsistent across deals and reps.
- Delivery teams reopen discovery work that should be complete pre-contract.
- Client confidence drops when teams ask repetitive onboarding questions.

## Root Causes
- Intake forms are optional or too generic for technical projects.
- Qualification criteria are not connected to implementation constraints.
- There is no structured handoff package shared with delivery at close.
- Teams optimize for deal speed, not implementation readiness.

## Step-by-Step Solution
1. Define mandatory intake fields for scope, systems, integrations, compliance, and deadlines.
2. Add conditional logic so high-risk project types trigger deeper qualification questions.
3. Generate a standardized handoff brief automatically when a deal reaches closed-won.
4. Route the brief to delivery with project risks, assumptions, and unresolved dependencies.
5. Introduce a readiness score that blocks kickoff scheduling below an agreed threshold.
6. Review lost time in kickoff phases monthly and refine intake rules accordingly.

## Time and Error Savings Estimate
Organizations that standardize automated intake usually reduce kickoff delays by 25-45% and cut avoidable re-discovery work by 30%+. In practice, that means faster time-to-value and fewer scope misunderstandings.

## FAQ
### Will this slow down sales velocity?
Not when designed well. Conditional questions only appear when needed, and strong intake prevents downstream delays that hurt revenue.

### What should the readiness score include?
Include scope clarity, stakeholder alignment, integration detail, timeline realism, and dependency risk.

### Who should own the handoff template?
Revenue operations should co-own it with delivery leadership so both conversion and execution quality stay balanced.
